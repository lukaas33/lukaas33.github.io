People have always thought they were somehow special. They assumed there was an invisible force making them different from the non-living. Slowly they found out that the difference between life and lifeless isn't that big. Life can appear from chemical reactions as proved in the Miller-Uray experiment.
In the 21st century we have discovered much about life. We are not that far away from creating life.
The life we create doesn't have to be biological. Simulated life will exist long before we create biological life. We already made much progress since the programs of Dawkins in 1980, but we still have a long way to go.


With this project I wanted to create simple, but lifelike, bacteria that adapted to the enviroment. I have done research on how to best simulate nature and research on bacteria and their evolution. But I have still made a lot of mistakes. These mistakes are mine and not my sources.
I was inspired to do this project by The Nature of Code (Daniel Shiffman). It's also where I learned how do use Vectors for movement.


The bacteria in my program undergo mutations which can be seen by the number of dots in their body. Their traits also change. They will be more or less adapted to the enviroment.
The enviroment has an effect on their energy use but luck is still a big factor in their survival.
A lot of info can be seen by clicking on a bacteria. You can view more data by typing global in the developer console.
